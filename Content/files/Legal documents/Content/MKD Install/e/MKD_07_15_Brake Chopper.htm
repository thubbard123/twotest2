Regen circuit

When the amount of returned energy from the -N builds the bus capacitor voltage up enough, the -C activates the regen chopper to start dumping the returned energy in the regen resistor (also called regenerative or brake resistor). An external regen resistor can be connected to -C X14.

Available external regen resistors are described in the Appendix (➜ # 1).

Functional description

When the amount of returned energy builds the bus capacitor voltage up enough, the -C activates the regen chopper to start dumping the returned energy in the regen resistor at the -C (connected to terminal X14). 90% of the combined power of all the coupled drives is always available for peak and continuous power.

The drive that has the lowest switch-off threshold (resulting from tolerances) reports an over-voltage fault if the DC bus threshold is exceeded. The drive power stage is disabled and the load coasts to a stop with the fault message “F501 Bus Over voltage" (➜ # 1). The -C fault relay contact (terminals X15A/1-2) is opened due to this fault.

Technical data

Observe the regeneration time (some minutes) after full load with peak regen power.
