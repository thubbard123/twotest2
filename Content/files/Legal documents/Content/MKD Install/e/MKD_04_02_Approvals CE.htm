Conformance with EU Low Voltage, EMC and RoHS Directives

EU Declarations of Conformity can be found on the Developer Network.

The devices have been tested by an authorized testing laboratory in a defined configuration, using the system components that are described in this documentation. Any divergence from the configuration and installation described in this documentation means that the user will be responsible for carrying out new measurements to ensure conformance with regulatory requirements.

declares the conformity of the modules with the following directives:

  • EC Directive 2014/35/EU, Low Voltage Directive
    Used harmonized standard EN 61800-5-1
  • EC Directive 2014/30/EU, EMC Directive
    Used harmonized standard EN 61800-3
  • EC Directive 2011/65/EU with delegated directive 2015/863/EU, RoHS Directive
    Used harmonized standard EN 50581

System -C with -N

  • -C must be powered from a 3 phase industrial supply network via a mains choke 3L.
  • The modules meet the noise immunity requirements to the 2nd environmental category (industrial environment).
  • For noise emission the system modules meet the requirement to a product of the category C2 (motor cable up to 10 m). With a motor cable length of 10 m or more, the system meets the requirement to the category C3.

These devices can cause high-frequency interferences in non industrial environments and may require measures for interference suppression (additional external EMC filters).
