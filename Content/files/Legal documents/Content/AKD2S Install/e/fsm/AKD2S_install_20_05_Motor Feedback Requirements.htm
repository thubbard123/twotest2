Motor feedback requirements

content required

Encoder and motor housing shall have a positive fit against each other or an adhesive shall be used between both housings. The fitting shall be over dimensioned by a factor of 20 to all breakaway torque values. The fatigue strength of the mechanical connection between the encoder shaft and the motor shaft must be shown to have a shaft breakage fault exclusion in accordance with IEC61800-5-2.

HIPERFACE DSL – Initial safety setup

During commissioning, the HIPERFACE-DSL encoder intended for safe feedback usage must be programmed by the SMM with a unique encoder index. This creates a strong connection between the physical encoder and the SMM to ensure, that the SMM communicates to the correct encoder even after a restart.

When a new HIPERFACE-DSL is connected to the drive and is configured as a safe feedback, the SMM will enter an I/O failure waiting for the user to acknowledge this I/O failure (using a safe input or FSoE). When the user acknowledges the error, the SMM will write a unique encoder index into the physical encoder.

  • HIPERFACE-DSL is now ready to operate as a safe feedback.
